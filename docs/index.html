W = Σi ci · e−λ·agei?
The weighted citation score (W) measures the strength of collaboration between two authors. For each co-authored paper i, the paper's citation count ci is multiplied by an exponential decay factor e−λ·agei, where agei is the number of years since publication and λ (lambda) controls how quickly older papers lose weight. A higher λ emphasises recent work; λ=0 treats all papers equally regardless of age. The edge score is the sum over all shared papers between the pair.
